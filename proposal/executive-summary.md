# Executive Summary

**Submitted to:** J. Okafor, Director of Procurement — Meridian Components, Inc.
**RFP Reference:** MC-2026-0417
**Submitted by:** Accenture
**Date:** April 27, 2026

---

Meridian Components has built a solid operational foundation with its inventory dashboard, but the current system is holding your team back. The Reports module has known defects that remain unresolved. The absence of automated tests has made your IT team reluctant to approve further changes — effectively freezing a system your operations team depends on daily. And the capabilities your Tokyo and London teams need are simply not there yet.

We understand this is not a greenfield project. It is a remediation and extension engagement on a system that was left incomplete. That distinction matters: it requires a vendor who will read the existing code carefully, fix what is broken correctly, and extend the system in a way that does not create new debt.

Our proposed approach addresses Meridian's requirements in order of the impact they unlock:

1. **Restore confidence in the system** by establishing automated end-to-end test coverage (R3) and resolving all outstanding defects in the Reports module (R1). Until these are in place, your IT team cannot safely approve changes and your operations team cannot trust what they see.

2. **Deliver the Restocking capability** (R2) that R. Tanaka's team has been waiting for — a purpose-built view that recommends purchase orders against live stock levels, demand forecasts, and a budget ceiling you control.

3. **Document the architecture** (R4) so that Meridian IT has a clear, current-state reference for everything we deliver.

We are proposing a fixed-fee engagement with phased delivery milestones so that Okafor's team has full cost visibility and Tanaka's team sees working software early.

Our assumptions and any open questions from the RFP are detailed in the Technical Approach section.
